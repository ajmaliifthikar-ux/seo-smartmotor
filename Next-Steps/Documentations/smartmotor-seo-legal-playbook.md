## 5. Recommended Legal / Policy Pages for SmartMotor.ae (UAE Context)

You are operating a UAE‑based digital service and collecting user data (contact forms, bookings, analytics). The UAE has a modern data protection framework (PDPL – Federal Decree‑Law No. 45 of 2021), consumer protection law (Federal Law No. 15 of 2020), and an e‑commerce / electronic transactions regime (Federal Decree‑Law No. 46 of 2021) that together regulate online services and consumer rights. [web:35][web:38][web:40][web:43]

**Strong recommendation:** work with a UAE‑qualified lawyer to draft and localise these pages. The notes below help you brief them and structure the content; they are not legal advice.

### 5.1 Privacy Policy (PDPL‑Aligned)

The UAE’s PDPL requires clear information on how you collect, use, store and share personal data and the rights of data subjects (access, correction, deletion, consent withdrawal, etc.). [web:40][web:43]

**Suggested sections for your Privacy Policy page:**

1. **Introduction & scope**  
   - State that the policy applies to all visitors and customers of smartmotor.ae and to data collected via website forms, WhatsApp links, call tracking, and analytics.

2. **What data you collect**  
   - Contact details (name, phone, email), vehicle information, booking details, and technical data (IP, browser, cookies).

3. **Legal basis & purpose of processing**  
   - Explain that you process data to respond to inquiries, provide services, manage bookings, send service reminders, and improve your website, in line with PDPL principles of consent, purpose limitation and minimal collection. [web:40][web:43]

4. **Data sharing & international transfers**  
   - Identify any third‑party tools used (hosting, email marketing, CRM, analytics) and whether data leaves the UAE, describing safeguards where applicable. [web:40]

5. **Data security measures**  
   - Describe technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss or misuse, aligned with UAE government guidance on privacy and security. [web:34][web:37][web:40]

6. **Data subject rights & contact details**  
   - Explain the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or object to processing, and provide a dedicated email/contact for privacy requests (e.g., privacy@smartmotor.ae or sales@smartmotor.ae). [web:40][web:43]

7. **Retention periods**  
   - Outline how long you keep contact/booking records and when they are anonymised or deleted.

8. **Cookies & tracking technologies**  
   - Briefly describe cookies and link to a more detailed Cookie Notice if you use advanced analytics/marketing tools.

9. **Updates to the policy**  
   - Reserve the right to update, with the last‑updated date clearly visible.

### 5.2 Terms & Conditions (Service and Website Use)

Under UAE consumer and e‑commerce laws, online traders must clearly set out key terms, prices, limitations, cancellation and refund rules, and must avoid “harmful terms” that unfairly restrict consumer rights. [web:35][web:38][web:41]

**Suggested sections for Terms & Conditions:**

1. **Definitions & scope**  
   - Define “Smart Motor Auto Repair”, “Customer”, “Website”, “Services”, etc.

2. **Booking & service process**  
   - Explain how customers can book online / by phone, how estimates are provided, and when a work order is considered accepted.

3. **Pricing, estimates & payments**  
   - Clarify that estimates may change if additional faults are found, but you will always seek customer approval; state accepted payment methods (cash, card, bank transfer, BNPL partners like Tabby). [web:1]

4. **Warranties & liability**  
   - Describe labour warranty (e.g., 6‑month labour warranty on repairs) and limitations, ensuring compliance with UAE consumer protection principles and avoiding clauses that waive essential rights or create “harmful terms.” [web:1][web:35][web:41]

5. **Customer obligations**  
   - Responsibilities such as providing accurate vehicle information, picking up vehicles promptly, and removing valuables.

6. **Cancellations, no‑shows & storage fees**  
   - Set reasonable rules for missed appointments and storage charges, ensuring they are not disproportionate penalties under the Consumer Protection Law. [web:35][web:41]

7. **Website acceptable use & IP**  
   - Basic rules for using content, logos, and images; prohibiting misuse or scraping.

8. **Governing law & jurisdiction**  
   - State that UAE law (and, if appropriate, the courts of Abu Dhabi) govern disputes.

### 5.3 Cookie Notice

If you use analytics, advertising pixels or remarketing tools, UAE data protection guidance recommends transparency about cookies and tracking. [web:37][web:40][web:43]

**Suggested sections:**

- What cookies are and why you use them (analytics, performance, marketing).
- Types of cookies used (session, persistent, first‑party, third‑party).
- A simple way for users to control cookies (browser settings or a consent banner tool).

### 5.4 Refund, Returns & Complaint Handling Policy (Online & Offline)

The Consumer Protection Law and e‑commerce regulations emphasise clear consumer rights to information, returns and complaints, especially for services sold online. [web:35][web:38][web:41]

For an auto repair workshop, you typically don’t have “returns” like e‑commerce products, but you should still explain:

- When re‑work or re‑inspection is provided under warranty (e.g., within 6 months for covered repairs). [web:1]
- How customers can raise a complaint (phone, email, in‑person, or via the relevant government authority).
- Expected response times and escalation steps.

### 5.5 Contact / Complaints Page

Have a dedicated **Contact & Complaints** page that:

- Lists all official contact channels (phone, WhatsApp, email, physical address, working hours).
- Explains how to raise a service problem and how Smart Motor will respond.
- Optionally links to relevant UAE government consumer portals for escalations, in line with transparency obligations. [web:37][web:41]
